Do Muslims Consume More or Less Porn on Ramadan?

Pornography and Ramadan may seem unrelated. However, these terms are very much linked both in real life and in Google searches. While pornography is always considered illicit in Islam, this is even more true over the month of Ramadan. The message of Ramadan emphasizes pious behaviors and abstaining from physical pleasures: specifically, sex, food and water during the daylight hours. Many Muslims increase their observance over this time-period through fasting and attending mosque.

Considering this, Muslims who ordinarily flout the pornography prohibition might consider cleaning up their act for the Ramadan month. All the while, many Muslim-majority countries are fighting a losing battle against rising consumption of pornography in their countries, with numerous governments attempting and failing to enforce internet porn bans.

There is plausible reason to assume a negative or positive impact.

On the one hand, Pornography consumption might be thought to decrease over Ramadan as individuals increase their religious observance and become occupied with family holiday celebrations. On the other hand, given that Ramadan is often marked by shorter work or school day in Muslim countries, individuals might gain more free time to search for pornography.

There has been conflicting research connecting pornography searching to religious observance and holidays. A selection of studies coducted at the individual level show that religious individuals report lower levels of porn consumption. Kyler Rasmussen, Alex Bierman. (2016) However, research relying on non-self-reported data from web subscriptions and google searches suggests the opposite.

In a U.S. study with google search data higher percentages of practicing Christians in a state were shown to predict higher frequencies of porn searching.(Andrew L. Whitehead & Samuel L. Perry 2018) A similar study showed that regions where more people report regularly attending religious services, purchased the same total level of porn but a smaller portion of subscriptions begin on Sunday. Edelman, B. (2009)

Finally, pornhub statistics from the holiday season demonstrate that traffic changes on Christmas Day vary widely by country. In some countries such as Australia, the UK, and the Italy there is a decrease in traffic by over 20%. However, in Japan, India and Russia there is the opposite effect of an increase in traffic from 2% to 13%.

Heres how I compiled my data

The dependent variable represents the mean popularity of the two most porn-related search terms, separately identified from Egypt, Tunisia, Libya, Arab Emirates, Saudi Arabia, Iran, Afghanistan, Jordan, Pakistan, Malaysia from Jan 5, 2014 through December 12, 2018. I begin the search after 2014 since google trends offers a convenient option to download weekly data for the past 5 years. If one downloads data from earlier, the data is measured in monthly instead of weekly increments, which wasnt fine-grained enough for my purposes. Since I was interested in change over time and not total volume, I tested and selected my terms on a country basis. For each country, I choose the two highest volume queries from a list of twenty, testing the Arabic and Persian lists in the following sources1, 2. In all but Iran and Afghanistan, the highest volume porn searches were for sex and porn, as opposed to Arabic language terms. This speaks to the international nature of the pornography industry. However, In Iran and Afghanistan, I replaced the term for porn since it was searched more popularly (the word is just a transliteration of sex in Perso-arabic alphabet).

A note on interpreting google trends values

Each data point is divided by the total searches of the geography and time range it represents to compare relative popularity. Otherwise, places with the most search volume would always be ranked highest. The resulting numbers are then scaled on a range of 0 to 100 based on a topics proportion to all searches on all topics. Different regions that show the same search interest for a term dont always have the same total search volumes. Source: Google trends

Independent Variables:

Presence of Ramadan is a binary variable with values of one or zero based on the presence of absence of Ramadan.

In Eid is a categorical variable which registers the presence of Eid al-Futr. Eid al-Futr is a three-day feasting holiday which always occurs immediately following Ramadan. Thus, In Eid is also standing as an after ramadan variable.

Week Before Ram is a binary variable indicating the week before Ramadan to test if individuals decrease their pornography searching in advance of the holiday.

*Controls: I include controls for year and country.

Findings

This study shows a negative relationship between the presence of Ramadan and porn searching in majority Muslim countries.

The presence of Ramadan is associated with a nine-percentage point decrease in weekly porn searches. As seen in regression 4, the week before Ramadan also results in a two-percentage point decrease in searches. However, in the week following, also known as Eid al-Futr, there is a rebound effect of an increase in pornography searches by over 15 percentage points which is greater in magnitude than the decrease (seen in regressions 3 and 4). My results are significant (p < .01) and I include controls for year and country.

Why does Pornography Consumption Decrease over the Holiday?

Decreases in the popularity of porn over Ramadan may correspond to levels of individual religious observance. However, there could also be social and scheduling factors associated with observance of Ramadan, which would alter individual practice.

Given that most pornography searches occur at night, chedule changes during Ramadan for late night family gatherings and breakfast meals and prayers likely render a significant impact to searching activities. Moreover, many countries have Ramadan laws, which criminalize public acts of non-observance, this could include viewing porn in public settings such as internet cafes.

Another factor could be the physical exhaustion from fasting decreases interest in sexual stimulation. Bio-chemical or neurological changes associated with fasting have been shown to decrease levels of libido and alter interest in pornographic content.

A Global Impact?

Given that Muslims make up about %24 percent of the global population, does the presence of Ramadan may have an impact on worldwide pornography searching?

To answer this I pull global google search data from from 2014-2018. We see that the presence of Ramadan is associated with a one and a half percentage point decrease in searches for sex and porn at .1 significance level, while controlling for year.

The Muslim population has been projected to increase by about 1.5% a year and by 2060 Muslims will constitute about %32 percent of the global population so their influence on Google trends might increase over time.
