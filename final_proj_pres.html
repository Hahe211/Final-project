A Country Level Analysis of the Impact of Ramadan on Pornography Googling

what is the impact of Ramadan on Pornography consumption in Muslim-majority countries? Pornography and Ramadan may seem unrelated. However, these terms are very much linked both in real life and in Google searches. While pornography is always considered illicit in Islam, this is even more true over the month of Ramadan. The message of Ramadan emphasizes pious behaviors and abtaining from physical pleasures: specifically sex, food and water during the daylight hours. Many musilms increase their observance over this time-period through fasting and attending mosque.

In light of this, Muslims who ordinarily flout the pornography prohibition might consider cleaning up their act for the Ramadan month. All the while, many Muslim-majority countries are fighting a losing battle against rising consumption of pornography in their countries, with many governments attempting and failing to enforce internet porn bans.

There is plausible reason to assume a negative or positive impact.

On the one hand, Pornography consumption might be thought to decrease over Ramadan as individuals increase their religious observance and become occupied with family holiday celebrations. On the other hand, given that Ramadan is often marked by shorter work or school day in Muslim countries, individuals might gain more free time to search for pornography.

There has been conflicting research connecting pornography searching to religious observance and holidays. A selection of studies coducted at the individual level show that religious individuals report lower levels of porn consumption. Kyler Rasmussen, Alex Bierman. (2016) However, research relying on non-self reported data from web subscriptions and internet search values suggests the opposite.

A state level study in the US demonstrates that higher percentages of practicing Christians in a state predict higher frequencies of porn searching. (Andrew L. Whitehead & Samuel L. Perry 2018) Other research demonstrated that regions where more people report regularly attending religious services, purchased the same total level of porn but a smaller portion of subscriptions begin on Sunday. Edelman, B. (2009).

Finally, pornhub publishes subscription statistics from the Christmas holiday season which indicate that traffic changes on Christmas Day vary widely by country. In some countries such Austrialia, the UK, and the Italy there is a decrease in traffic by over 20%. However, in Japan, India and Russia there is the opposite effect of an increase in traffic running from 2% to 13%.

This study shows a strong negative correlation between the presence of Ramadan and porn searching in majority Muslim countries. The presence of Ramadan in a Muslim country causes a decrease in the frequency of sex and porn related searches. Moreover, there is a ‘rebound effect’ of a 10% increase on Eid al-Futr the celebratory holiday following Ramadan.

Why the Dip?

Dips in porn consumption over Ramadan may correspond to levels of individual religious observance. However, there could also be social factors of stigma associated with non-observance of Ramadan, which would alter individual practice.

Given that most pornography searches occur at night schedule changes during Ramadan and late family gatherings likely render a significant impact to searching activities. Additionally, bio-chemical or neurological changes associated with fasting have been shown to decrease levels of libido and alter interest in pornographic content. Moreover, many countries have Ramadan laws, which criminalize public acts of non-observance.
